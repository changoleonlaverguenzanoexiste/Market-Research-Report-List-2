<p><p>Bioactive glass ceramics are a type of biocompatible material used in various medical and dental applications. They are commonly composed of a combination of MgO, CaO, SiO2, and P2O5, along with other trace elements. These elements work together to promote bone growth and integration with surrounding tissue. The MgO component helps enhance mechanical properties, while CaO and SiO2 play a role in bioactivity. P2O5 helps improve bioresorbability, allowing for gradual replacement by natural bone tissue. Other trace elements are included for specific functionalities based on the intended application.</p></p>
