<p><p>Fire resistant fluids can be categorized into four main types: high water fluids, water glycol fluids, synthetic fire resistant fluids, and other fluids. High water fluids contain more than 35% water and are commonly used in hydraulic systems. Water glycol fluids are a mixture of water, glycol, and other additives, offering high fire resistance. Synthetic fire resistant fluids are made from synthetic materials and provide excellent thermal stability. Other fluids include phosphate esters, silicone-based fluids, and vegetable oils, each offering unique properties for specific applications.</p></p>
