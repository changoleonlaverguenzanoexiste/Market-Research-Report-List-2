<p><p>The dyes and organic pigments market can be divided into different types based on their chemical properties and applications. Disperse dyes are used for coloring synthetic fibers, while reactive dyes form covalent bonds with the substrate, making them ideal for cotton and other cellulose fibers. Vat dyes are insoluble in water and require a reducing agent to become soluble, commonly used for cotton dyeing. Other types of dyes include acid dyes, basic dyes, direct dyes, and solvent dyes, each with unique characteristics and applications.</p></p>
