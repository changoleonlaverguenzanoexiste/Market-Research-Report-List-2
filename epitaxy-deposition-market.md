<p><ul><li>MOCVD</li><li>Molecular Beam Epitaxy</li><li>Other CVD Epitaxy</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Epitaxy deposition refers to the process of growing thin films on a substrate to create semiconductor devices. There are various types of epitaxy deposition methods, including Metal Organic Chemical Vapor Deposition (MOCVD), Molecular Beam Epitaxy (MBE), and other Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) techniques. MOCVD involves the use of metal organic precursors, MBE uses molecular beams to deposit materials, and other CVD epitaxy methods involve different chemical processes to achieve thin film growth. Each of these techniques has its unique advantages and applications in the semiconductor industry.</p></p>
