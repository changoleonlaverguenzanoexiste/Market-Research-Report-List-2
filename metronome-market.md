<p><p>Mechanical metronomes are traditional, non-electronic devices that use a pendulum or weighted arm to produce a ticking sound at a set tempo. They are popular among musicians for their classic look and reliable performance. Digital metronomes, on the other hand, are electronic devices that use LED displays and buttons to set and adjust tempo settings. They are favored for their accuracy, versatility, and additional features such as different time signatures and rhythm patterns. Both types cater to musicians of all levels and preferences.</p></p>
