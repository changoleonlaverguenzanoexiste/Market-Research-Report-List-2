<p><p>Occupancy sensors are widely used in lighting systems to detect the presence of people in a space and turn the lights on or off accordingly. The market is divided into different types such as PIR (Passive Infrared) sensors, Ultrasonic sensors, Dual-technology occupancy sensors, and others. PIR sensors detect body heat, while ultrasonic sensors emit sound waves to detect motion. Dual-technology sensors combine both methods for enhanced accuracy. Other types of sensors may include microwave or camera-based technologies for occupancy detection.</p></p>
