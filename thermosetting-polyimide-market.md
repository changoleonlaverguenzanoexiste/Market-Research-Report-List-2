<p><p>Thermosetting polyimide is a high-performance polymer that can be processed through various methods such as extrusion molding, hot compression molding, direct forming, isotactic pressing, and other specialized processes. Extrusion molding involves pushing the molten polyimide through a die to create specific shapes. Hot compression molding uses heat and pressure to mold the polyimide into desired forms. Direct forming is a process where the polyimide is directly shaped without the use of a mold. Isostatic pressing involves compressing the polyimide powder at high pressure to form a dense structure. Other specialized processes include techniques such as resin transfer molding or vacuum bag molding.</p></p>
