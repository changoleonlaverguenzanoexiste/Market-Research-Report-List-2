<p><p>Refrigerated benchtop centrifuges are commonly categorized into two types based on their speed capabilities: low speed and high speed. Low speed centrifuges are designed for applications such as separating blood samples and cell suspensions, typically reaching speeds of up to 5,000 rpm. On the other hand, high speed centrifuges are capable of reaching speeds exceeding 20,000 rpm, making them suitable for applications such as isolating subcellular components or DNA/RNA purification. Both types offer refrigeration capabilities to maintain sample integrity during centrifugation.</p></p>
